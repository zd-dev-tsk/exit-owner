The model, transparently

  • Post-money SAFE: ownership = amount ÷ post-money cap, fixed after all SAFEs convert and before new round money (YC semantics — stacked SAFE percentages are additive and dilute only existing holders).
  • Pre-money SAFE: cap-based ownership ≈ amount ÷ (cap + all converting SAFE amounts).
  • Discount: ownership = amount ÷ ((1 − discount) × round pre-money); the SAFE converts at whichever basis gives more ownership.
  • Priced round: new investors get amount ÷ (pre-money + amount). Pre-money is inclusive of converted SAFEs and the expanded pool (the standard, founder-unfriendly convention).
  • Pool top-up: raised to the target % of post-round fully-diluted, carved out pre-money — existing holders absorb it, new investors don't.
  • Exit: every investor holds a 1x non-participating preference and takes the greater of their money back or pro-rata, solved iteratively.

This is an educational model, not legal or financial advice. Real documents control; have a lawyer read yours.

FAQ

How much do stacked SAFEs dilute founders?

Post-money SAFEs are additive: three $500k SAFEs at a $5M cap each take 10% — 30% combined — all out of the founders' side of the table. This is the single most common surprise at the first priced round.

Why did my ownership drop more than the round size?

Option pool top-ups are usually created pre-money, so founders absorb both the new investors' stake and the entire pool increase.

Why do I get less than my percentage at exit?

1x non-participating preferences mean investors take their money back first in low exits. A founder with 40% of a company sold for less than total capital raised can walk away with very little.
